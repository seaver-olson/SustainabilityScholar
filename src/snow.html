The Importance of Snow

According to the national weather service, snow is converted to water at a 10:1 ratio
This means that the grand walls of snow after crazy blizzard become only a few inches of water!
for example 2 feet of snow, or 24 inches, will melt into about 2.4 inches of snow later.

Snow is very important for our ecosystem as the
snowmelt, or the water that is collected from snow, represents a large portion of our drinking water
as well as our livestock water

Streams and rivers are also fed water through the snow that melts on and near them!
