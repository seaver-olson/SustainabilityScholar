Biodiversity Basics

Biodiversity refers to the variety of life on Earth, including plants, animals, and microorganisms, as well as the ecosystems in which they live. It is a measure of the health of ecosystems and is essential for the survival of all living organisms, including humans. Biodiversity can be observed at different levels, including genetic diversity, species diversity, and ecosystem diversity.

Importance

Biodiversity is important for many reasons. It provides ecosystem services that are essential for human well-being, such as clean air and water, pollination of crops, and climate regulation. Biodiversity also supports the economy through industries such as agriculture, forestry, and tourism. Additionally, biodiversity has intrinsic value and provides cultural and aesthetic benefits to humans.

Threats

Biodiversity is facing numerous threats, primarily due to human activities. Habitat destruction, pollution, climate change, overexploitation of natural resources, and invasive species are some of the major threats to biodiversity. These threats are causing species extinctions at an alarming rate and are leading to the degradation of ecosystems around the world.
